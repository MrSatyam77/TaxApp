Why do I need to verify my EFIN?
Protecting your EFIN account and your taxpayer data is as important to us as it is to you. As such, we require that all ERO 's (Electronic Return Originators) provide verification of their EFIN (E-File Identification Number) before e-filing. It's an important first step in preventing tax-related identify theft and fraud.
Requirements for EFIN Verification with the IRS:
1. One EFIN Per Firm Physical Location (as defined in IRS Pub. 3112).
2. If more than one Firm shares a single Physical Location, Each Firm must have a separate Software License and a separate EFIN.
3. Firm Name (DBA Name), Firm Physical Address, EFIN, and ERO Contact on the EFIN Application must match Software Licensed Firm Name and Physical Address, EFIN, and Contact Name.
What to Submit for EFIN Verification:
Submit the 'Application Summary' from the IRS e-Services website.
On the left you can see an example of the Application Summary. Click here for instructions on how to access and print the Application Summary on the IRS e-Services site.
Caution: EFIN letters older than 12 months from the date they were generated will be rejected.
What NOT to Submit. We do not accept any of the following forms as EFIN verification:
Your PTIN Registration
Your original Form 8633 Application
Your EFIN Activity Report
Your EIN Tax ID number
Your e-Services Account Registration Information
